The Big E

USS Enterprise CV-6

The USS Enterprise (CV-6), nicknamed "The Big E," was the seventh U.S. Navy vessel to bear the name. It was a Yorktown-class aircraft carrier and one of the most decorated ships of World War II. Commissioned in 1938, she was relocated to the pacific. When the Japanese attacked pearl harbor on December 1941, she served the war, being able to sink her first Japanese submarine HIJMS I-70. USS Enterprise played a pivotal role in many significant battles such as the Battle of Midway and the Battle of Leyte Gulf, the largest naval battle in history.

General Information

Fate : Scrapped

Key Events

Pearl Harbor: Although not present during the attack on Pearl Harbor, USS Enterprise was instrumental in subsequent Pacific operations

Doolittle Raid: USS Enterprise provided air support for the Doolittle Raid on Tokyo in April 1942.

Battle of Midway: She played a crucial role in the decisive Battle of Midway in June 1942, which turned the tide of the war in the Pacific.

Guadalcanal Campaign: She supported numerous operations in the Solomon Islands, including the Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands.

Late War: Participated in multiple battles across the Pacific, including the battles at the Philippine Sea, Leyte Gulf, and the Battle of Okinawa

Post-War: Decommissioned in February 1947 and eventually scrapped in 1958.

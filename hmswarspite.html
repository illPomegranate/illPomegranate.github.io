The Grand Old Lady

HMS Warspite

HMS Warspite was the seventh ship to bear the name. Commissioned in 1915, she was one of the oldest Warship to have fought in the war. The motto "Belli Dura despicio" meaning "I despise the hardships of war." was the ships famous quote embedded into the crest of the ship. She fought in multiple campaigns throughout ww2 including the norwegian campaign and the battle of normandy. Her service in the Mediterranean during ww2 was one of the most helpful and advantageous in the victory of the Allies, Her accurate gunnery, resilience and critical role in supporting the operations gave her the reputation of being one of the most formidable battleships of her time.

General Information

Name : Warspite
Class : Queen Elizabeth-class battleship
Identification : 03
Launched : 26 November 1913
Commissioned : 8 March 1915
Nicknames : The Grand Old Lady
Awards : 15 battle honors (10 inherited honours)
Armament : 4 × twin 15 in (381 mm) guns
14 × single 6 in (152 mm) guns
2 × single 3 in (76 mm) AA guns
4 × 21 in (533 mm) torpedo tubes
Fate : Scrapped

Key Events

Battle of Calabria : Warspite participated in the Battle of Calabria, the first major naval engagement between the British Mediterranean Fleet and the Italian Regia Marina.

Battle of Cape Matapan : Warspite, along with other British ships, engaged Italian cruisers and destroyers, contributing to the victory. It was a decisive naval battle where the British Royal Navy defeated the Italian fleet, preventing them from disrupting Allied convoys to Greec

Invasion of Sicily: Warspite provided extensive naval gunfire support to Allied forces during the invasion of Sicily, targeting coastal defenses and enemy positions. Her accurate fire helped clear the way for the amphibious landings and provided cover for advancing Allied troops.

Salerno Landings: Warspite participated in the amphibious assault on the beaches of Salerno, Italy, providing fire support to Allied troops under heavy German counterattacks.

Normandy Landings : Warspite provided fire support during the Allied invasion of Normandy, bombarding German coastal defenses to assist the amphibious landings.

Final Operations: She continued to serve in the Mediterranean sea until the end of the war.
